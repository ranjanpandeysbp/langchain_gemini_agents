Rules of Cricket

Cricket is a bat-and-ball sport played between two teams of eleven players. The game centers around two batsmen and eleven fielders. The basic objective is to score more runs than the opposing team while minimizing the opposition's scoring opportunities.

A cricket match consists of innings where batsmen take turns hitting a bowled ball. The bowler delivers the ball from one end of the pitch toward a batter at the opposite end. The batter attempts to hit the ball and score runs by running between two sets of wickets. Each completed run equals one point.

Key rules include: A batter is out if the bowler hits the stumps (wickets), if a fielder catches a hit ball, or through leg-before-wicket (LBW). The batting team continues until ten batsmen are out. Matches are played in formats like Test cricket lasting five days, One Day Internationals featuring 50 overs per side, and Twenty20 matches with 20 overs each.

Fielding positions vary strategically around the oval field. The captain decides field placement and bowling strategy. Umpires enforce rules and make critical decisions. Fair play and spirit of the game are fundamental principles governing cricket worldwide. Understanding these rules is essential for enjoying this globally beloved sport played across continents.
